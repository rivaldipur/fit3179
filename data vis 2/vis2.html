Life Expectancies Around The World

Every country has a different living circumstances. Life expectancy is affected by many varaibles such as health and income. This whole visualization shows the relations of such factors on life expectancies globally.

Economic, Population, and Life Expectancy

Europe, which has the highest life expectancy on average, also have the highest GDP per Capita. Meanwhile, African nations have both lowest GDP per Capita and life expectancy on average.

Urban Population & Life Expectancy

Higher urbanization is linked to longer life, particularly in developed European and Asian nations, while African countries often show the opposite.

Health & Life Expectancy

Healthcare Cost & Experts

The Healthcare Cost-Physician Ratio measures physician availability and healthcare costs in a population's lifetime.

In general, the higher the better.

European countries overall have highest ratio along with some Asian countries.

Countries in North America, South America, Australia, and most Asian sit in the middle.

African countries have the lowest ratio which also reflects onto the life expectancies.

Maternal Mortality

Maternal mortality rates are often associated with limited healthcare access, especially during pregnancy and childbirth.

Countries with lower maternal mortality rates have higher life expectancies like Australia and many European countries
